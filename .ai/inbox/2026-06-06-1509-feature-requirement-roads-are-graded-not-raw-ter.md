(feature/requirement) ROADS ARE GRADED, not raw terrain. In the terrain model (R052), a road/lane is NOT just terrain_height sampled along its centreline (raw follow) — it is a GRADED CORRIDOR: its own cut/fill profile with a BOUNDED slope (engineered grade, max gradient), and the terrain is cut/filled to MEET the road (the road corridor is a first-class graded footprint, same cut/fill-to-a-pad primitive as blocks/lots, but a continuous ribbon with a smoothed bounded-slope longitudinal profile + cross-slope/crown). So roads never step over every terrace or pitch undrivably steep; cuts through hills, embankments/fills across dips. Updates R052 §3.7/P4: lane Y = graded profile (bounded |slope|), retaining/embankment derived from pad_y(road) - terrain along the corridor. Project: HOD.
